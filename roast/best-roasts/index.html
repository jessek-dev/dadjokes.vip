Savage Roasts for Any Friend

Not every roast needs a name. The best generic roasts target universal truths — the friend who's always late, the one who thinks they're funny, or the buddy who peaked in high school. The trick is specificity: the more specific the roast, the harder it lands.

That said, if you really want to make it personal, use their name. A name-based roast feels custom-made even when it takes zero effort. Our roast generator creates savage, personalized roasts for any name in seconds.

Some approaches that always work:

  • The comparison roast: Compare them to a famous person with the same name — then highlight everything they're not. ("Named after Alexander the Great... and that's where the similarities end.")
  • The name pun: Find the hidden word in their name and build the roast around it. Amanda? A-man-duh. Athena? A-thena take a hint.
  • The spelling roast: Point out how their name is spelled, mispronounced, or confused with something else.
  • The resume roast: Read their name like a headline, then deliver the disappointing news underneath.

How to Deliver the Perfect Roast

A great roast isn't just about the words — it's about the delivery. Here's how to land it every time:

  • Timing is everything. The best roasts come out of nowhere. Don't announce "I'm about to roast you." Just drop it casually mid-conversation for maximum impact.
  • Keep it playful. Your tone should say "I love you" even when your words say "you're a disaster." If the person being roasted isn't laughing, you've gone too far.
  • Know your audience. Some friends can take heavy fire. Others need a gentler touch. Read the room before you light it up.
  • Commit to the bit. Don't laugh at your own roast before you finish delivering it. Deadpan delivery makes good roasts great.
  • Have a follow-up ready. The best roasters always have a second line loaded. When they recover from the first hit, drop the second.
  • End on a high note. After the roast, give them a genuine compliment. The contrast makes both the roast and the compliment hit harder.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the best roasts for friends?
The best roasts for friends are personalized, playful, and based on something specific like their name, a shared experience, or an inside joke. Name-based roasts are the easiest to deliver because everyone has a name, and they feel custom without requiring deep knowledge.
How do I roast someone without being mean?
Keep it playful, not personal. Target things that are obviously silly (like name puns) rather than real insecurities. The golden rule: if you wouldn't say it in front of their mom, it's probably too far. A good roast makes everyone laugh, including the person being roasted.
